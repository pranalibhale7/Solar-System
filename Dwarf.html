DWARF PLANETS

Ceres

The only dwarf planet located in the inner solar system.

The only dwarf planet in the asteroid belt. It may hide a salty ocean beneath its surface. Ceres was the first dwarf planet ever visited by a spacecraft (Dawn).

Pluto

Pluto was long considered our solar system's ninth planet.

Tiny but famous, once a planet! It has five moons, with Charon being its largest. Pluto's heart-shaped ice region makes it look like a cosmic Valentine.

Haumea

Haumea is one of the fastest rotating large objects in our solar system.

Looks like a stretched football spinning fast. Its rapid rotation gives it an unusual shape. Haumea also has a ring and two tiny moons.

Makemake

Makemake is the second-brightest object in the Kuiper Belt .

Bright and icy, hiding in the Kuiper Belt. No known atmosphere, just a frozen desert. Its reddish hue hints at organic compounds on the surface.
