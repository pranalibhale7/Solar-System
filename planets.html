PLANETS

Mercury

The fastest planet, racing around the Sun in just 88 days.

Mercury has extreme temperature swings, from burning hot days to freezing nights, because it has almost no atmosphere to trap heat.

Venus

A scorching world wrapped in thick, toxic clouds.

Venus spins backward compared to most planets and is hotter than Mercury, thanks to a runaway greenhouse effect caused by dense carbon dioxide clouds.

Earth

The only known planet that supports life.

Earth’s liquid water, protective atmosphere, and balanced climate make it uniquely suited for life as we know it in the vastness of space.

Mars

The red planet shaped by dust, ice, and ancient rivers.

Mars shows strong evidence of having once held flowing water and may have supported microbial life billions of years ago.

Jupiter

The giant planet with a storm bigger than Earth.

Jupiter’s Great Red Spot is a massive storm that has raged for centuries, and its strong gravity protects inner planets by deflecting asteroids.

Saturn

Famous for its breathtaking rings of ice and rock.

Saturn’s rings are made of billions of icy particles, ranging from tiny grains to massive chunks, orbiting the planet in perfect harmony.

Uranus

An ice giant that spins on its side.

Uranus has extreme seasons lasting decades, as its tilted axis causes one pole to face the Sun for long periods while the other remains in darkness.

Neptune

A deep blue world with the fastest winds in the Solar System.

Neptune’s winds can reach speeds over 2,000 km/h, making it the windiest planet ever observed, despite its vast distance from the Sun.
